Biology × Engineering × Code
Our platform isn't just a chemical test; it's a multiplexed information system. By encoding antibiotic resistance data into DNA barcodes, we bridge the gap between biological events and digital-grade precision.

Key Features

  • Rapid Detection: Results in under 1 hour.
  • High Specificity: Multiplexed DNA barcodes for accurate differentiation.
  • Field Ready: Low-cost and requires no complex lab infrastructure.

AMR-DNA Probe System

Flagship Project: A novel chemistry-driven diagnostic for rapid β-lactamase detection through programmable DNA barcodes.

How It Works

  • 01 Conjugation: Each probe is tuned for a specific class of resistance enzyme via its C-7 side chain, conjugated to a unique DNA barcode.
  • 02 Trigger: Enzymatic hydrolysis of the antibiotic ring triggers a self-immolative release, freeing the specific DNA barcode into the sample.
  • 03 Readout: Free barcodes hybridize to spatially separated capture zones on a lateral flow strip.

Seamless Implementation

Needing no complex machinery, just human-centric engineering.

  1. Collect: Small volume of Blood or Sputum.
  2. Lysis: Mix with reagent to release bacterial enzymes.
  3. Incubate: Wait for probe cleavage & activation.
  4. Read: Visual pattern readout on lateral flow.
